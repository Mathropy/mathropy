There is an equilateral triangle with a side length of $2$. Additionally, you have several rectangles, each with a width of $1$ and a height of $2-\sqrt{3}$. The total area of the equilateral triangle is exactly enough to hold up to 6 of these rectangles. However, they won't fit perfectly in their original shapes. To fit all 6 rectangles into the triangle, you must cut them into smaller pieces.

**The Goal**: What is the minimum number of pieces required to fit all 6 rectangles into the equilateral triangle? If the six rectangles remain uncut, they count as 6 pieces.

**Rules**:
- You can cut the rectangles into any shape.
- The pieces can be rotated or reflected (flipped) to fit into the triangle.

The diagram below demonstrates that by cutting 4 rectangles into 5 pieces, they can be fitted into the equilateral triangle. Is it possible to fit all four rectangles into the triangle without cutting any of them at all?
